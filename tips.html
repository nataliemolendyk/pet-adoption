Pet Care Tips

Helpful advice for new and prospective pet owners to ensure a happy, healthy life for your new companion.

Preparing Your Home for a New Pet

Before bringing your new pet home, take time to prepare your space:

  • Pet-proof your home: Secure loose wires, remove toxic plants, and store chemicals out of reach.
  • Create a safe zone: Set up a quiet area with a bed, water bowl, and toys where your pet can retreat.
  • Stock up on essentials: Food and water bowls, collar and leash, ID tags, bed, crate (if using), toys, and grooming supplies.
  • Choose the right food: Ask the shelter what food your pet has been eating and stick with it initially to avoid stomach upset.
  • Schedule a vet visit: Book a checkup within the first week to establish care and discuss vaccinations.

Your First Week with a New Pet

The first week is about building trust and establishing routines:

  • Go slow: Give your pet space to explore at their own pace. Don't overwhelm them with too many visitors.
  • Establish a routine: Consistent feeding times, walk schedules, and bedtime help pets feel secure.
  • Start training gently: Begin with basic commands like "sit" and "stay" using positive reinforcement.
  • Monitor eating and drinking: Some pets may be nervous eaters at first. Contact your vet if they refuse food for more than 24 hours.

Pet Nutrition Basics

Proper nutrition is key to your pet's health and longevity:

  • Quality food matters: Look for AAFCO-approved pet foods with real meat as the first ingredient.
  • Age-appropriate diet: Puppies/kittens, adults, and seniors have different nutritional needs.
  • Portion control: Follow feeding guidelines and avoid free-feeding to prevent obesity.
  • Fresh water always: Ensure clean, fresh water is available at all times.
  • Avoid toxic foods: Never feed pets chocolate, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic, or xylitol (artificial sweetener).

Training Tips

Training strengthens your bond and ensures good behavior:

  • Use positive reinforcement: Reward good behavior with treats, praise, and play. Never use punishment.
  • Keep sessions short: 5-10 minute training sessions are more effective than long ones.
  • Be consistent: Use the same commands and rules from every family member.
  • Socialize early: Expose your pet to different people, animals, and environments in a controlled, positive way.
  • Consider professional help: Group classes or private trainers can help with specific behavioral issues.

Vet Care & Vaccinations

Regular veterinary care keeps your pet healthy:

  • Annual checkups: Even healthy pets need yearly exams to catch issues early.
  • Core vaccinations: Dogs need rabies, distemper, parvovirus, and adenovirus. Cats need rabies, feline distemper, and feline herpesvirus.
  • Parasite prevention: Year-round flea, tick, and heartworm prevention is essential.
  • Dental care: Brush your pet's teeth regularly and schedule professional cleanings as recommended.
  • Spay/neuter: This prevents health issues and unwanted litters. Most adopted pets are already altered.
